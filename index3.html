A Rapidly Expanding Inferno and Its Containment

The Camp Fire, ignited on November 8, 2018, swiftly became California’s most destructive wildfire, destroying 18,793 structures. By November 15, over 5,500 firefighters and extensive resources were deployed as the fire spread to 146,000 acres. Rainfall on November 21 significantly aided containment, which reached 85 percent. By November 25, the fire was fully contained after 17 days of intense firefighting efforts.

Environmental Impacts

The Camp Fire caused severe air pollution affecting the San Francisco Bay Area and Central Valley, leading to school closures and hazy conditions reaching as far as New York City. Recovery efforts were hampered by the need to test burned debris for hazardous materials, with advisories issued against re-habitation due to health risks. Contaminated drinking water systems posed significant challenges, prompting educational efforts and workshops to address post-wildfire public health issues.
