The Camp Fire of 2018 was the deadliest and most destructive wildfire in California's history. Sparked on November 8th near the town of Paradise, the fire spread rapidly due to high winds and dry conditions, engulfing entire communities. Within hours, Paradise was nearly wiped off the map, with 85 people losing their lives and tens of thousands fleeing for safety. Environmental impacts

The Camp Fire led to severe air pollution across the San Francisco Bay Area and Central Valley, resulting in widespread school closures and hazy conditions even reaching New York City. Recovery efforts faced delays due to testing for hazardous materials in burned debris, with advisories against re-habitation issued due to health risks. Contaminated drinking water systems and significant challenges in water safety were revealed, prompting educational efforts and workshops to address post-wildfire public health issues.
